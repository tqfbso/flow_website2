The innovation leader's primary tool is clear awareness of what is actually happening.

"Most innovation leaders are solving yesterday's problems with tomorrow's solutions. Present moment leaders solve today's reality with today's resources."

  • Internal Resistance Intelligence: Early warning systems for organizational antibodies

04

Distributed Authority

Enable breakthrough decisions at the edges where market reality is clearest and action is fastest.

Core Practice

Traditional innovation requires too many approvals from people too far from market reality. Distributed authority pushes decision-making power to the edges where customer insight is freshest and response time is fastest. Create clear boundaries, then unleash intelligent action within them.

"The closer decision-makers are to customer pain, the better their innovation choices become."

Business Application

  • Customer-Proximate Teams: Embed innovation teams directly with customer-facing operations
  • Bounded Autonomy Frameworks: Clear guardrails with maximum decision authority within bounds
  • Real-Time Resource Allocation: Budget authority at team level for rapid iteration cycles
  • Front-Line Innovation Councils: Customer service and sales teams driving product evolution

What This Eliminates

‚ùå Innovation decisions made by committees far from customer reality
‚ùå Approval bottlenecks that kill market timing
‚ùå Innovation teams waiting for permission to solve obvious problems
‚ùå Executive innovation theater divorced from operational reality
